Homemade Italian Meatballs

It seems that pretty much every Italian family had their own recipe for meatballs. In my family, we like ours big with extra fennel. The same simple ingredients apply to any traditional Italian meatballs, but my family chooses to exclude veal from our recipe. These babies (more like toddlers because of the size) are an accompaniment to Sunday sauce and are rarely, if ever, served separately. Dabble some of that sauce on top of pasta with a couple of your giant meatballs and you have a meal so legendary it could be requested on Death Row.

Ingredients

  • one packet of ground beef
  • one packet of ground pork
  • 3 eggs + 1 egg yolk
  • 1 1/2 cups of seasoned bread cumb
  • 3/4 cup of parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon of onion powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon of garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons of fennel

Directions

Combine all ingredients into one large bowl. Mix well and into balls of your desired size. Fry in vegetable oil until browned on all sides and still pink in the middle. Put balls into a baking pan with parchment paper and sprinkle with parmesean cheese.
