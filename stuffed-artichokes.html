Stuffed Artichokes

A lot of people underestimate the versatility of the artichoke. They are usually only sourced for their hearts which can be used on salads, in dips, and on pizza. Well, let me turn you on to stuffed artichokes. Anyone who enjoys ditching their utensils for some good old-fashioned finger food will love these. I, for one, have always loved peeling apart each leaf one by one and scraping off all that delicious stuffing with my teeth. Thereâ€™s just something so satisfying about it. This recipe is fairly simple, but makes for a great meal all on its own.

This recipe will stuff 3-4 large artichokes.

Ingredients

  • 1 package of sausage crumples
  • 6 cloves of minced garlic
  • 2 1/2 cups of seasoned bread crumb
  • 1 cup of parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup of chopped fresh parsley

Directions

Sautee minced garlic and sausage on a pan until cooked. Then, in a large mixing bowl combine all of your ingredients and mix together well.

Trim the lips of the artichoke leaves with a scissor and cut the stems evenly so they can sit up straight. Then,spread the leaves open as much as possible being careful not to break them. Stuff the artichoke leaves with your stuffing mixture getting it into as many crevices as possible.

Fill a large sauce pot with 2 inch deep water. Place the stuffed artichokes into the pot and sprinkle lemon juice and pasmesan cheese on the tops. Cook on medium to low heat with a cover on for as long is it takes for the leaves to begin to soften. Be sure the water doesn't entirely evaporate and add more if it does. The longer they can cook the better they will be. Cook for a minimum for 4 hours.
