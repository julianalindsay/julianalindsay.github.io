Sunday Sauce

In my family Sunday dinner is not something that is optional. We have always felt that it is necessary to gather once a week and keep family ties strong. Ever since my grandmother and grandfather passed away two years ago, Sunday dinner traditions have become more difficult to keep on the calendar. However, despite the grandkids getting older and the matriarch of the family being gone, Sunday dinner is something that will always keep us together. Waking up to the smell of a giant homemade pot of sauce just hits different - there’s nothing like it! So, whether you call it sauce or gravy, this staple item is something that is non negotiable for family get-togethers. The portion sizes for this recipe are large enough to feed an entire family; and I’m not just talking about mom, dad and kids. I’m talking about aunts, uncles, grandparents, and grandkids.

Because I have made this recipe so many times at this point, I don't typically use exact measurements. Instead, once you have the taste for it you're pretty much able to eye most of the ingredients. For the purpose of storing this recipe in my virtual filing cabinet I have written out exact measurements.

Pro Tip! The earlier you start your sauce, the better flavor profile you will develop and the thicker your sauce will be.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans of Cento's peeled whole plum tomatoes
  • 2 cans of Cento's tomato sauce
  • 1 can of tomato paste
  • 3 cloves of minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of dried basil
  • 1 tablespoon of salt
  • 1/2 tablespoon of pepper
  • 1/2 tablespoon of oregano
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 cup of heavy cream or regular milk (optional)
  • 3 tablespoons of sugar (optional)
  • 1/2 cup of parmesean

Directions

Saute garlic and onion with a tablespoon of oilive oil in the bottom of a large sauce pot. Add canned whole tomatoes and tomato paste. Let them soften and smash with potato masher until smooth. Then, add your other cans of tomato sauce. Add seasonings and continue to stir. Let simmer for atleast 4 hours before adding milk and sugar.

Add homemade meatballs in for extra flavor and continue to simmer for another 2 hours. Taste often and add seasonings to your liking
