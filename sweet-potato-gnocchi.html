Hearty Sweet Potato Gnocchi

People tend to think that homemade pasta is too out of reach for a typical weeknight dinner. However, this super simple sweet potato gnocchi will certainly change their minds. With just two ingredients, it’s not only easy but it tastes absolutely incredible!

Keep in mind these little balls of deliciousness are extremely filling and extra hardy, making this a perfect dish for wintertime. I usually prepare my sweet potato gnocchi with a simple pink sauce, but you might get the best flavor experience using a lighter butter sauce.

This recipe may make more dough than you need so you can freeze the dough whole or finish off your gnocchis and then save them uncooked in the fridge.

Ingredients

  • 2 large sweet potatoes
  • 2 1/2 cups of flower + more for rolling out dough
  • 1/2 tablespoon of salt + more for pasta water

Directions

Cook the sweet potatoes in the oven for 40 minutes at 350°F. Make sure to poke holes in the skin. Once sweet potatoes are cooled, remove the skin and place the insides into a bowl. Using a fork, mash the sweet potato into a paste until it’s as smooth as possible. At this point, mix in the salt.

On a large cutting board or clean kitchen counter, make the flour into a mound with an indent at the top. Place the sweet potato mash into the indent and combine until you have a smooth dough ball. Separate the dough ball into four smaller dough balls. One at a time, roll the balls into long cylinders. Cut into about 1 inch portions. Dump gnocchi into bowling salt water and remove once floating. Serve with your favorite sauce and you’re done!
